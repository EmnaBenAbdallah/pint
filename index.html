Introduction

The Process Hitting is a recently introduced formalism suited for modelling qualitative dynamics of biological networks. It is a special class of Asynchronous Automata Networks.

The Process Hitting comes with powerful static analysis for dynamical properties, including fix points, successive reachability properties, and cut sets for reachability. Such analysis are tractable for analysing very large-scale networks, ranging from hundreds to thousands of concurrently interacting components. The translation to related formalisms, in particular Boolean and multi-valued networks, is also possible.

Pint implements the language, simulation, formal analysis, and translation of Process Hitting models.
